A quick summary

Sounds of Chinatown is a collaborative, community-focused website and project that aims to record the unique sounds of New York City’s Manhattan Chinatown. Our self-guided "Sound Walks" offer short (15-20 min) guided walking paths that explore the acoustic environment of Chinatown. Our site also features an interactive Chinatown Sound Map [link] archiving the sounds of this unique neighborhood, to document the past and present sounds and noises of Chinatown. See the map here [link] and find instructions here on how you can submit your own audio recordings to be included on the site! This site is a means of thinking about Chinatown’s unique neighborhood in a different way. Our goal in creating these features is to draw wider attention to sound and noise as important factors that affecting individual and community health.

About

our mission

The sounds and noises in urban environments paint a picture and contributes to our memories about a specific place or time. Sounds of Chinatown aims to create a living record of the sounds that add to the unique culture and makeup of New York City’s Manhattan Chinatown community – recorded and described by those who encounter it. This platform is a space for visitors to listen to and to share their various experiences in and with Chinatown through the perspective of sound. We hope that the sound experiences and data found on this site will also help to draw attention to issues related to the impact of urban noise on Chinatown residents. The Sounds of Chinatown website is a collaboration between researchers from the Center for the Study of Asian American Health (CSAAH) at the NYU Grossman School of Medicine, NYU Tandon’s Center for Urban Science and Progress (CUSP), and community stakeholders in New York’s Manhattan Chinatown, including residents of Chung Pak LDC, students with Immigrant Social Services (ISS), and… local individuals from the community like you!

site description

Manhattan Chinatown is a thriving neighborhood district within a vibrant and constantly changing city. Local residents and visitors to the area are exposed to a daily variety of lively and bustling sights, smells, and sounds as they walk through its streets. Chinatown is also a neighborhood exposed to frequent change as generations move in or grow and move away, or when older shops or businesses are replaced by newer ones, or when buildings are torn down only to be replaced by taller ones. Chinatown is also located in the center of downtown Manhattan, serving as a major truck route, with the areas around Canal Street and Manhattan Bridge being some of the noisiest in the city. In dense, urban environments such as Manhattan’s Chinatown, the impact of city sounds on residents living near each other and alongside major streets that make up this neighborhood is hard to ignore. What is more, being exposed to loud noises may have long-term health impact such as causing hearing damage – but noise does not have to be loud in order to be harmful to health. Exposure to any noise for a lengthy amount of time can affect a person’s quality of life and mental well-being. Take a look at our Sound Walks [link] and data collected about the daily amount of noise that takes place at a central building in Manhattan Chinatown [link] to learn more about how sounds (and noise) acts as a factor in how a community lives. How You Can Participate

Sound Walks

CSAAH and their partners have created several self-guided walking tours, called Sound Walks, to encourage community individuals to explore the acoustic environment of the Chinatown neighborhood. Whether you are a curious local or are a visitor who is not able to travel to New York City right now, our Sound Walks offer a close, first-hand experience of the sights, sounds, and character of this historic neighborhood – through your headphones! The Team

NYU CSAAH

The NYU Center for the Study of Asian American Health (CSAAH) is a National Institutes of Health (NIH) National Institute on Minority Health and Health Disparities (NIMHD) funded National Research Center of Excellence. CSAAH is based in the Section for Health Equity within the Department of Population Health at NYU Grossman School of Medicine. Established in 2003 through an NIH NCMHD Project EXPORT (Excellence in Partnership, Outreach, Research, and Training) Center grant, CSAAH is the only Center of its kind in the country that is solely dedicated to research and evaluation on Asian American health and health disparities. CSAAH is committed to identifying Asian American, Native Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander health priorities and reducing health disparities by integrating and building on the work of researchers and over 75 Asian American community, government, business and academic/medical partners.

NYU Tandon’s Center for Urban Science and Progress (CUSP) – SONYC

NYU CSAAH is partnering with Chung Pak LDC, researchers from NYU Tandon’s Center for Urban Science and Progress (CUSP) and other local individual and community partners in Manhattan’s Chinatown to draw attention to a key health concern affecting this community’s health: noise.
